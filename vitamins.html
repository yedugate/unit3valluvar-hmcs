1) Definition & Biochemistry

Vitamins are organic compounds required in small amounts for growth, metabolism, and health.

  • They do not provide energy, but act as coenzymes and regulators in biochemical reactions.
  • Most vitamins cannot be synthesized by the body (exceptions: Vitamin D from sunlight; Vitamin K & B12 from gut bacteria).

4) Water‑Soluble Vitamins

Vitamin C (Ascorbic Acid)

  • Sources: Citrus fruits, amla, guava, tomato.
  • Functions: Collagen formation, wound healing, antioxidant, immunity.
  • Deficiency: Scurvy (bleeding gums, delayed healing).
  • Excess: Diarrhea, kidney stones.
  • RDA: ~75–90 mg/day.

✅ Quick Recap for Students

  • Vitamins = organic regulators (not energy‑yielding).
  • Fat‑soluble (A, D, E, K): stored; can be toxic in excess.
  • Water‑soluble (C & B‑complex): not stored; need daily intake.
  • B‑complex = 8 vitamins that act as coenzymes.
  • Classic deficiencies: B1 → Beri‑beri, B3 → Pellagra, C → Scurvy, D → Rickets.
