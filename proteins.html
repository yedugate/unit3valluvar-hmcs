Proteins – Body Builders

11th–12th grade • Definition & basics • Amino acids • Classification • Sources & functions • One digestion diagram • Printable

Definition

Proteins are organic compounds made of amino acids, containing Carbon (C), Hydrogen (H), Oxygen (O), and Nitrogen (N) (sometimes Sulfur and Phosphorus). They are called body‑building foods because they help in growth and repair.

Amino acids Peptide bonds Growth & repair

1) Biochemical Basics

  • Proteins = chains of amino acids joined by peptide bonds.
  • Amino acids are the building blocks of proteins.

Essential vs Non‑essential Amino Acids

GroupMeaningExamples
EssentialMust come from foodLysine, Methionine, Tryptophan, Leucine, Isoleucine, Valine, Threonine, Phenylalanine, Histidine*
Non‑essentialBody can synthesizeAlanine, Aspartic acid, Glutamic acid, Serine, Glycine, Proline, Tyrosine**

*Histidine is essential in infants; **Tyrosine becomes essential if phenylalanine is low.

Protein Quality (Quick)

ConceptSimple Note
Complete proteinsContain all essential amino acids in good amounts (e.g., egg, milk, fish)
ComplementationCombine cereals + pulses to improve amino acid balance (e.g., rice + dal)

2) Classification of Proteins (Basic)

TypeBasisExamples
Simple proteinsYield only amino acids on hydrolysisAlbumin (egg white), Globulin (plasma protein)
Conjugated proteinsProtein + non‑protein part (prosthetic group)Hemoglobin (heme + globin), Lipoprotein
Derived proteinsBreakdown products of simple/complex proteinsPeptones, Proteoses

3) Sources of Proteins

CategoryExamples
Animal sources (rich in essential AAs)Milk, eggs, fish, chicken, meat
Plant sourcesPulses, soybean, groundnut, cereals, nuts

4) Functions of Proteins

FunctionExplanation (Simple)
Growth & repairBuild muscles, bones, tissues
Enzymes & hormonesSpeed reactions, regulate body functions
ImmunityAntibodies are proteins
TransportHemoglobin carries oxygen
Energy (last resort)~4 kcal/g when carbs/fats are insufficient

5) Health Aspects

AspectDetails
DeficiencyKwashiorkor (edema, skin/hair changes), Marasmus (severe wasting, stunted growth)
ExcessKidney overload, dehydration, bone calcium loss
RDA0.8–1 g/kg body weight (~50–60 g/day for adults)

Balance sources: mix plant and animal proteins or use cereal‑pulse complementation.

Quick Recap for Students

  • Proteins = chains of amino acids (building blocks of life).
  • Classification: Simple, Conjugated, Derived.
  • Sources: milk, pulses, eggs, fish, soybean.
  • Functions: growth, repair, enzymes, hormones, immunity, transport.
  • Deficiency: Kwashiorkor, Marasmus; RDA ~ 0.8–1 g/kg.
