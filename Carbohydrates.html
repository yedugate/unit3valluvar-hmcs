Carbohydrates – Energy Givers

11th–12th grade • Biochemical classification • Student‑friendly tables • One digestion diagram • Printable

Definition

Carbohydrates are organic compounds made of C, H, and O in the ratio (CH2O)n. They are the main energy source of our body (~4 kcal/g).

Energy Protein‑sparing Fat metabolism

1) Biochemical Classification of Carbohydrates

Type Composition Examples Notes (Simple)
Monosaccharides (single sugar) One sugar unit Glucose (grape sugar), Fructose (fruit sugar), Galactose Quickest source of energy
Disaccharides (two sugars) Two monosaccharides Sucrose (cane), Lactose (milk), Maltose (malt) Easily digested
Polysaccharides (many sugars) Long chains of monosaccharides Starch (plants), Glycogen (animals), Cellulose (plants) Stored energy, dietary fiber

2) Sources of Carbohydrates

CategoryExamples
CerealsRice, wheat, maize, barley
Roots & TubersPotato, tapioca, yam
FruitsBanana, mango, grapes
SugarsCane sugar, honey
OthersMilk (lactose)

3) Functions of Carbohydrates

FunctionExplanation (Simple)
EnergyMain source of energy (~4 kcal/g)
Protein sparingSaves proteins from being used for energy
Fat metabolismNeeded to burn fat properly (avoid ketosis)
StorageStored as glycogen in liver & muscles
FiberCellulose adds bulk → prevents constipation

4) Health Aspects

ConditionEffect
DeficiencyWeakness, fatigue, ketosis
ExcessObesity, diabetes, dental caries
RDA55–65% of daily calories (~250–350 g/day for adults)

Choose whole grains & fruits; limit refined sugar and sweetened drinks.

Quick Recap for Students

  • Carbohydrates = main fuel of life (~4 kcal/g).
  • Classes: Monosaccharides, Disaccharides, Polysaccharides.
  • Sources: cereals, roots/tubers, fruits, sugars, milk.
  • Functions: energy, protein‑sparing, fat metabolism, fiber for gut health.
  • Health: deficiency → fatigue; excess → obesity, diabetes.
