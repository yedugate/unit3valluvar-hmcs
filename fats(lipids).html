Fats (Lipids) – Energy Reserves

11th–12th grade • Definition & basics • Biochemistry • Classification • Sources & functions • One digestion diagram • Printable

Definition

Fats are organic compounds made of Carbon (C), Hydrogen (H), and Oxygen (O), sometimes with Phosphorus (P) or Nitrogen (N).

  • Provide concentrated energy (~9 kcal/g).
  • Stored as triglycerides (fatty acids + glycerol) in adipose tissue.
  • Essential for energy storage, insulation, and vitamin absorption.
9 kcal/g Triglycerides A, D, E, K absorption

1) Biochemical Basics of Fats

  • Building blocks: Fatty acids + Glycerol.
  • Fatty acids may be:
    • Saturated – no double bond; usually solid at room temperature (e.g., ghee, butter).
    • Unsaturated – one/more double bonds; usually liquid oils (e.g., sunflower oil, fish oil).
  • Special lipids: Phospholipids (cell membranes), Sterols (cholesterol, hormones).

2) Classification of Fats (Basic)

TypeDescriptionExamples
Simple lipidsEsters of fatty acids with alcohol (glycerol)Fats, oils
Compound lipidsFat + another group (P, protein, carbohydrate)Phospholipids, Lipoproteins

3) Sources of Fats

CategoryExamples
Animal sourcesGhee, butter, meat fat, fish oil, egg yolk
Plant sourcesGroundnut oil, coconut oil, sunflower oil, mustard oil, nuts, seeds

Tip: Prefer unsaturated fats (nuts, seeds, fish, plant oils); limit trans fats.

4) Functions of Fats

FunctionExplanation (Simple)
Energy~9 kcal/g → about double that of carbs/proteins
StorageStored as triglycerides in adipose tissue
InsulationProtects against heat loss; cushions organs
AbsorptionNeeded for fat‑soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K)
Structural rolePhospholipids form cell membranes
HormonesSteroid hormones & bile salts derived from cholesterol

5) Health Aspects

AspectDetails
DeficiencyPoor growth, dry skin, infertility, vitamin A/D/E/K deficiency
ExcessObesity, atherosclerosis, heart disease, hypertension
RDA20–30% of daily calories (~30–50 g/day)

Quick Recap for Students

  • Fats = energy‑dense (~9 kcal/g) from fatty acids + glycerol.
  • Classes: Simple, Compound, Derived lipids.
  • Sources: oils, ghee, nuts, fish, butter.
  • Functions: energy, storage, insulation, vitamin absorption, hormones, membranes.
  • Health: deficiency → dry skin, fat‑soluble vitamin deficiency; excess → obesity/heart disease.
