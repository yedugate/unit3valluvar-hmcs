Water & Fiber – Vital but Non‑Energy Nutrients

11th–12th grade • Definition & basics • Biochemistry • Sources & functions • One digestion/absorption diagram • Printable

1) Water – The Universal Solvent

Definition & Biochemistry

  • Water = H2O, the most abundant molecule in the body (~60–70% of body weight).
  • Present as intracellular and extracellular fluids.
  • Medium for virtually all biochemical reactions.

Sources

Drinking water, milk, fruits (melon, orange), vegetables (cucumber, tomato).

Functions

FunctionExplanation (Simple)
SolventDissolves nutrients; enzymes work in water
TransportCarries nutrients & waste in blood/urine
Temperature controlSweating helps maintain body heat
LubricationJoint fluid, saliva, digestive juices
MetabolismRequired for hydrolysis reactions

Deficiency

Dehydration → thirst, fatigue, kidney stones.

Excess

Water intoxication → electrolyte imbalance.

RDA

~2–3 liters/day (varies with age, climate, activity).

2) Fiber (Dietary Fiber / Roughage)

Definition & Biochemistry

  • Non‑digestible carbohydrates in plant foods.
  • Human enzymes cannot break them; some are fermented by gut bacteria.
  • Types:
    • Soluble fiber – dissolves in water, forms gel (e.g., pectin, gums).
    • Insoluble fiber – adds bulk to stool (e.g., cellulose, lignin).

Sources

Whole grains (brown rice, oats), legumes, fruits (apple, banana), vegetables (carrot, cabbage), nuts & seeds.

Functions

FunctionExplanation (Simple)
Digestion aidAdds bulk → prevents constipation
Heart healthLowers cholesterol
Diabetes controlSlows sugar absorption
Colon healthReduces colon cancer risk
Weight controlGives satiety (feeling of fullness)

Deficiency

Constipation, piles, increased risk of colon cancer.

Excess

Bloating, diarrhea, reduced absorption of calcium/iron/zinc.

RDA

~25–40 g/day for adults.

Single diagram showing the different fates of water and dietary fiber in the gut.

✅ Quick Recap for Students

  • Water → life solvent (~2–3 L/day); deficiency causes dehydration.
  • Fiber → non‑digestible carbs; keeps gut healthy (~25–40 g/day).
  • Both are non‑energy nutrients but essential for overall health.
