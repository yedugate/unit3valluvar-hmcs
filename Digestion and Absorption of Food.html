Digestion and Absorption of Food

11th–12th grade • Step-by-step process • Clear enzyme tables • Simple terms • One flow diagram • Printable

1) What is Digestion?

Digestion is the process of breaking down complex food (carbohydrates, proteins, fats) into simple absorbable forms.

  • Carbohydrates → Glucose
  • Proteins → Amino acids
  • Fats → Fatty acids + Glycerol

2) What is Absorption?

Absorption is the process by which digested nutrients pass into the blood or lymph through the walls of the small intestine (villi).

3) Steps of Digestion in Humans

Large Intestine No enzymes – Water, minerals, vitamins absorbed Waste formed (feces)

✅ Quick Recap for Students

  • Digestion = breakdown; Absorption = uptake into blood/lymph.
  • Major site of absorption = small intestine (villi).
  • End products: Glucose, Amino acids, Fatty acids + Glycerol, Vitamins, Minerals, Water.
