Nutrition Classification

Student‑friendly notes (11th–12th): clear tables + a digestion‑linked diagram

Nutrition can be classified by how organisms obtain food: two main types → Autotrophic and Heterotrophic.

Autotrophs → make food Heterotrophs → depend on others

1) Autotrophic Nutrition

  • Organisms make their own food from simple raw materials.
  • Use CO₂, H₂O and energy (sunlight or chemical energy).
  • Examples: Green plants (photosynthesis), some bacteria (chemosynthesis).
SubtypeHow Food is ProducedExamples
PhotosynthesisLight energy + CO₂ + H₂O → glucose (food)Green plants, algae
ChemosynthesisEnergy from chemical reactions → foodNitrifying & sulfur bacteria

2) Heterotrophic Nutrition

  • Organisms depend on others for ready‑made food.
  • They cannot synthesize food from raw materials.
TypeMeaning (Simplified)Examples
HolozoicTake solid food → ingestion → digestion → absorptionHumans, cow, lion
SaprophyticAbsorb nutrients from dead & decaying matterFungi, mushrooms
ParasiticLive on/in a host and take its foodTapeworm, lice, Cuscuta

✅ Quick Recap

  • Autotrophs make food (photosynthesis or chemosynthesis).
  • Heterotrophs depend on others: holozoic, saprophytic, parasitic.

Nutrient Classification

11th–12th grade • Student‑friendly tables • One digestion/absorption diagram • Printable

Nutrients are chemical substances in food required for energy, growth, repair, and regulation. They are grouped into Macronutrients and Micronutrients.

1) Main Classification of Nutrients

Category Nutrients Included Needed in… Function (Simple)
Macronutrients Carbohydrates, Proteins, Fats, Water, Fiber Large amounts (grams/day) Give energy & build the body
Micronutrients Vitamins, Minerals Small amounts (mg or µg/day) Regulate body functions, keep us healthy

2) Macronutrients – “Energy & Building”

Nutrient Sources Main Role Deficiency Excess
Carbohydrates Rice, wheat, potato, fruits Quick energy Fatigue, weakness Obesity, diabetes
Proteins Pulses, milk, eggs, fish Body‑building, repair Kwashiorkor, Marasmus Kidney burden
Fats Oils, ghee, nuts, fish Energy store, insulation, vitamin absorption Poor growth, dry skin Heart disease, obesity
Water Drinking water, fruits, vegetables Solvent, transport, temperature control Dehydration Water intoxication
Fiber Whole grains, vegetables, fruits Adds bulk, prevents constipation Constipation Bloating, mineral loss

3) Micronutrients – “Regulation & Protection”

Nutrient Sources Main Role Deficiency Disease
Vitamin A Carrot, papaya, fish oil Good vision, skin health Night blindness
Vitamin D Sunlight, milk, fish Bone health Rickets, Osteomalacia
Vitamin C Citrus fruits, amla Healing, immunity Scurvy
Vitamin B‑complex Cereals, pulses, eggs Energy metabolism, nerves Beri‑beri, Pellagra
Calcium Milk, ragi, greens Bones, teeth Rickets, Osteoporosis
Iron Liver, spinach, dates Hemoglobin (blood) Anemia
Iodine Iodized salt, sea foods Thyroid function Goiter

✅ Quick Recap for Students

  • Macronutrients → needed in large amounts → provide energy & building blocks.
  • Micronutrients → needed in small amounts → help in regulation & protection.
  • A balanced diet includes both types.
